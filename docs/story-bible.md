# Story Bible

## Working Title

After Hours Market

## Premise

The player runs a small neighborhood shop where regular customers bring ordinary needs, private worries, and fragments of local gossip. As days pass, small inconsistencies suggest the town is stranger than it first appears.

## Tone

- Cozy, lightly strange, and character-driven.
- Grounded in shop routines, money pressure, and neighborhood relationships.
- Mystery should enter gradually through details, rumors, and repeated oddities.
- Avoid horror shock value in the first prototype.

## Setting

A compact neighborhood with an old market, nearby apartments, a laundromat, bus stops, and small businesses that have seen better decades. The shop feels familiar to locals but slightly out of time after closing.

## Player Character

The player character runs the shop and is trying to keep it alive. They are practical, observant, and tired enough to miss things until the pattern becomes impossible to ignore.

Open questions:

- Did they inherit the shop, buy it, or take over temporarily?
- Are they returning to the neighborhood or new to it?
- What debt, promise, or personal reason keeps them here?

## Early Customer Cast

### Mrs. Vale

A regular who buys tea, batteries, and gossip. She notices everything but rarely says exactly what she means.

### Nico

A nervous teen who runs errands for family members and sometimes asks for items that do not appear on the shelves until he names them.

### Rafi

A delivery driver who knows the neighborhood's rhythms and warns the player when those rhythms change.

### The Late Customer

A quiet customer who appears near closing. Their requests should feel almost ordinary, but not quite.

## Day 1 Story Beat

The day should establish normal shop life first. The strange element can be a single late request, a rumor, or an item appearing where it should not be.
